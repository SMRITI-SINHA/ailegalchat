2.0 Template: Counter Statement

The Counter Statement, or Written Statement of Defence, is a critical pleading in civil litigation. Its legal function is to serve as the defendant's or respondent's formal, paragraph-by-paragraph rebuttal to the allegations made in a plaintiff's plaint or a petitioner's affidavit. This document is the primary vehicle for presenting the opposing party's defense, allowing them to admit, deny, or clarify each factual averment made by the petitioner. A robust Counter Statement not only traverses the opponent's claims but also introduces the respondent's version of events and raises legal objections, thereby framing the contested issues that the court will adjudicate.

2.3 Paragraph-wise Reply to Affidavit/Plaint

1. The averments in para 1 of the affidavit that [summarize the petitioner's primary allegation, e.g., the petitioner is a beneficiary under a registered Will dated 19.02.2024 executed by the 6th defendant] are all stoutly and categorically denied as false.

2. The averments in para 2 of the affidavit that [summarize any admitted facts, e.g., the petitioner filed the suit for the reliefs detailed in para 3 (a) to (g)] are admitted as correct.

3. The averments in para 3 of the affidavit are stoutly and categorically denied as false. This respondent submits that the alleged Will dated 19.02.2024 is not genuine and has been created only for the purpose of the case. The petitioner has not approached this Hon’ble court with clean hands.

4. This respondent submits that the averments in the affidavit itself disclose that the 6th defendant did not file a written statement for want of physical and mental fitness. Whileso, it is totally unbelievable that the 6th defendant is said to have executed a Will in favour of the petitioner.

5. This respondent further submits that the present application, seeking to [state the relief sought by the petitioner], shows collusion between the petitioner and other parties. The documents produced along with the petition all seem to be created falsely only for the purpose of the case, and this respondent does not admit the genuineness of any of the said documents.

2.4 Concluding Prayer

Therefore, it is prayed that this Hon’ble Court may be pleased to dismiss the petition with costs and thus render justice.
